Lessons Learned and Continuing Challenges

Recruitment and Retention

Challenge: Competing with other extracurriculars at Isenberg.

Solutions:

  • Require completion of the Insurance Foundations program for analytics eligibility.
  • Offer exclusive swag for special projects or leadership roles.
  • Provide academic credit for second-year independent study projects.
  • Award five scholarships to e-board members (CEO, COO, CPO, CCO, CMO).

Student Communication with Partners

Challenge: Inconsistent communication due to younger leadership.

Solution: Co-directors will handle partner communications, allowing students to focus on smaller tasks to build confidence.

Leadership Development

Solution: Sara will prioritize leadership training, while Carey refines the academic curriculum. Early training will improve retention.

Next Steps

  • Recruitment: Robust fall strategy with tabling, club fairs, class announcements, and Freshman Transitions Program collaboration.
  • Leadership Development: Target early-career students, provide coaching, and create standard operating procedures.
  • Analytics: Continue industry-based projects in premium prediction and fraud detection (Spring 2025).
  • Industry Collaboration: Strengthen partnerships through advisory roles, guest speaking, and applied projects.
  • Student Engagement: Support students through a four-year path from club involvement to full-time placements.
  • Long-Term Growth: Secure funding for faculty initiatives, research, and resources.
